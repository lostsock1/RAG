## Introduction

This appendix presents current population estimates for selected countries across all continents. Data are drawn from the United Nations Department of Economic and Social Affairs (UN DESA) World Population Prospects and supplemented by national census offices where available. All figures represent mid-year estimates for 2024 unless otherwise noted.

Population data are essential for understanding demographic trends, planning public services, allocating resources, and analyzing economic development patterns. The countries listed below were selected to represent a range of population sizes, geographic regions, and economic development levels.

## Notes on Data Quality

Population figures are estimates and subject to uncertainty. Factors affecting accuracy include:

- **Census frequency:** Some countries conduct censuses every 10 years; others less frequently. Intercensal estimates rely on statistical models.
- **Registration systems:** Not all countries maintain comprehensive vital registration systems (births, deaths, migration).
- **Migration:** Rapid migration flows can quickly change population figures between census years.
- **Definitions:** Population counts may include or exclude certain groups (e.g., military personnel abroad, undocumented residents) depending on the source.

## Liechtenstein: A Closer Look

**Liechtenstein** is a small, doubly landlocked principality located between Switzerland and Austria in the Alps. With a population of **39,584** (2024 estimate) and an area of just 160 km², it is one of the smallest countries in Europe. Despite its size, Liechtenstein has one of the highest GDP per capita figures in the world, driven by a strong financial services sector and diversified industrial base.

Key demographic features of Liechtenstein:

- **Population density:** 247.4 per km² — relatively high for a mountainous country
- **Urbanization:** Approximately 85% of the population lives in urban areas
- **Life expectancy:** 83.4 years (among the highest globally)
- **Fertility rate:** 1.47 children per woman (below replacement level)
- **Foreign-born population:** Approximately 34% of residents are foreign nationals, primarily from Switzerland, Austria, and Germany
- **Language:** German (official), with Alemannic dialects in everyday use
- **Currency:** Swiss franc (CHF)

Liechtenstein is a constitutional monarchy headed by the Prince of Liechtenstein. It is a member of the United Nations, the European Free Trade Association (EFTA), and the Schengen Area, though it is not a member of the European Union.
